serendipity

Word Origin

From Serendip, an old name for Sri Lanka + -ity. Coined by Horace Walpole in 1754 based on the Persian fairy tale "The Three Princes of Serendip," whose heroes "were always making discoveries, by accidents and sagacity, of things which they were not in quest of."
